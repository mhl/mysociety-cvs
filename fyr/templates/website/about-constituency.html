This page has been produced with assistance and advice from the Electoral Commission, including their leaflet, Electoral Registration in Great Britain

Representation of Servicemen

As a serviceman, you are still entitled to representation as a Service Voter. If you've not already registered, you need to fill out the form from http://www.aboutmyvote.co.uk/vote/register/?defCountry=5 - a part of the Electoral Commission. You can use a UK address with which you are affiliated to. That will be your constituency.

Representation to Ex-Patriots

Ex-Patriots need to have been registered to vote in the UK at some point within the last 15 years. You should use the address with which you are most familiar in the UK.

Representation of Crown Servants

As a Crown Servant/British Council Employee, you are entitled to representation. If you've not already registered, you need to fill out the form from http://www.aboutmyvote.co.uk/vote/register/?defCountry=4 - a part of the Electoral Commission. You can use a UK address with which you are affiliated to. That will be your constituency.

Representation of the Homeless, those of No Fixed Abode, Remand Prisioners or Mental Hospital Patients

So long as you have an address at which you have spent "considerable time" for 3 months, you're eligible to register to vote: your local ward and constituency will come from the post-code of the address you are familiar with. The form for registering to vote is available from http://www.aboutmyvote.co.uk/vote/register/?defCountry=6
